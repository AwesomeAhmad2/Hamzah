Creativity

The part of CAS that deals with being creative. Visual arts, exploration, and raising fundraisers are all parts of creativity. It is the part where the student uses creative thinking to help create a better society.

Action

The part of CAS, which includes taking action to create a better society. The activity must include physical movement for a healthier lifestyle. An example would be going to the gym for the first time in a while.

Service

The part of CAS that involves voluntary work for the community. In order for it to be count, the service must be voluntary and beneficial for the student. A great example would be picking up litter from public parks in your community.

Alliance Francaise:

Creativity

Learning Outcome:

Tuesday, September 11, 2018

Over the summer, I registered in a French course at Alliance Francaise in the Diplomatic Quarter. I would go there to acquire knowledge in the French language and meet other peeople who have different goals than me. One of the reasons I wanted to learn a new language is because I believe in Sapir-Whorf Hypothesis. The hypothesis states that different languages create different ways of thinking. However, there were other reasons why I wanted to learn French.

From this experience, I gained many IB learner profiles. The most notable one was communicator, for the reason that I gained relationships with my classmates and learned the language by communicating in class. After the 7 week course, I felt my perspective of the world changed, which indicates that Sapir-Whorf hypothesis is correct. Aside from the language itself, I learned about the French culture and how they live their daily lives.

Saudi National Day:

Service

Learning Outcome:

Thursday, September 27, 2018

Today, we celebrated the 88th Saudi National day in school. As part of the celebration, our superintendent invited students from another school that were suffering from down-syndrome. I volunteered to aid in greeting, welcoming, and making sure the down-syndrome students did what they were supposed to do. When they arrived, I tried to greet them with a big smile and kind words. Then, we helped them sit down in their authorized seats.

After my interactions with the children, I realized the blessing I was in as I began to reflect on the experience. I felt somewhat guilty because they did nothing to wrong to get diseased and I did nothing to deserve to not get diseased. Another learner profile I used was caring. Whenever I interacted with them, I tried to show how much I care about them. A specific incident I remember would be when I saw one of the students who appeared to be lost, so I helped him find his friends.

A Walk in the Park:

Creativity

Learning Outcome:

Monday, November 12, 2018

This week in school, our language and literature A teacher decided to take our lesson for the day to a nearby park. Me along with the teacher and the other students headed out to the park in the beginning of class taking only our books, which we are reading in class and is called "1984 by George Orwell". On our way to the park, my classmate Nawaf gave us a fast summary about the events that happened in Chapter 5. Then, when we arrived at the park we all split up and read Chapter 6 in the peace of the park.

After returning back to school, I decided to write a CAS reflection on this experience. I believe that some this experience I gained values that weren't present in me before. For example, I never realized how satisfying it is to interact with nature and how peaceful it is to read a book while hearing the chirping of the birds. I displayed a very important IB learner profile, which was being openminded about the creation of nature. In my opinion, we, as a commnuity, should contemplate the beautiful nature around us.

Trip to Al-Baha

Creativity

Learning Outcome:

Sunday, November 18, 2018

Last weekend, my older brothers and I traveled to Al-Baha to attend our friend's wedding. We arrived Friday afternoon and as soon as we landed at the airport, we realized the beauty of nature in Al-Baha. As we drove to the hotel, I continued to admire the beauty of the Hejaz Mountains as well as take a couple of pictures here and there. In the evening, we drove to the wedding. As soon as we arrived, we understood the difference of traditions in Al-Baha and other regions in the Kingdom. The next day we visited toursit attractions such as Dhey Al Ain Village and Al Raghdan Forest Park.

While reflecting on the trip, i realized that this experience changed me as a person. First of all, I displayed and developed many of my IB learner profiles. I was openminded during the wedding towards the traditions of the people of Al-Baha. Also, I was reflective about how God was able to create such beautiful mountains and how nature works; from the trees to the mountains to the bees. Finally, I was principled because I visited public property that cannot be fixed if it were to be damaged.

Action

While in our beach house in Jeddah, we rented bikes. My cousins and I rented bikes and started racing around the compounds. Hiking in the Diplomatic Quarter

Action

Learning Outcome:

Wednesday, January 30, 2019

My classmate and I acted as student leaders during a Grade 9 field trip to the Diplomatic Quarter. The field trip was a hike around the diplomatic quarter's beautiful parks and roundabouts. However, we faced some problems during trip. An officer of the Diplomatic Security Forces stopped us to interrogate. He asked us if we had permission to be in the diplomatic quarters and due to some confusion, our permission form was lost. Since, both the teachers with us didn't speak Arabic, I was the person communicating to with the officer. After 15 minutes of interrogation, we continued our path to iHop, where we had breakfast and then headed back to school.

Throughout the field trip, I displayed my leadership in a variety of ways. First of all, I was leading the group through the hike with my classmate. Since both our instructors hadn't been on the trip before, we were given the opportunity to lead the group throughout our journey. I also displayed my leadership qualities when we were being interrogated by the Diplomatic Security Forces officer. I acted as a translater between the teachers incharge and the officer while explaining to the officer the purpose of the trip and our final destination.
