Creating a Better Society

Welcome to the CAS Project of Hamzah Kurdi. On this website, you will be able to discover my adventures through a 18 month period through my reflections and my final CAS project.

  • Learn more

Creativity

The part of CAS that deals with being creative. Visual arts, exploration, and raising fundraisers are all parts of creativity. It is the part where the student uses creative thinking to help create a better society.

Activity

The part of CAS, which includes taking action to create a better society. The activity must include physical movement for a healthier lifestyle. An example would be going to the gym for the first time in a while.

Service

The part of CAS that involves voluntary work for the community. In order for it to be count, the service must be voluntary and beneficial for the student. A great example would be picking up litter from public parks in your community.

What I've Achieved

The following are my CAS reflections starting from September 2018 to March 2020

Creativity

Tuesday, September 11, 2018

Over the summer, i registered in a French course at Alliance Francaise in the Diplomatic Quarter. I would go there to acquire knowledge in the French language and meet other peeople who have different goals than me. One of the reasons i wanted to learn a new language is because i believe in Sapir-Whorf Hypothesis. The hypothesis states that different languages create different ways of thinking. However, there were other reasons why i wanted to learn French.

From this experience, i gained many IB learner profiles. The most notable one was communicator, for the reason that i gained relationships with my classmates and learned the language by communicating in class. After the 7 week course, i felt my perspective of the world changed, which indicates that Sapir-Whorf hypothesis is correct. Aside from the language itself, i learned about the French culture and how they live their daily lives.

Service

Thursday, September 27, 2018

Today, we celebrated the 88th Saudi National day in school. As part of the celebration, our superintendent invited students from another school that were suffering from down-syndrome. I volunteered to aid in greeting, welcoming, and making sure the down-syndrome students did what they were supposed to do. When they arrived, i tried to greet them with a big smile and kind words. Then, we helped them sit down in their authorized seats.

After my interactions with the children, i realized the blessing i was in as i began to reflect on the experience. I felt somewhat guilty because they did nothing to wrong to get diseased and i did nothing to deserve to not get diseased. Another learner profile i used was caring. Whenever i interacted with them, i tried to show how much i care about them. A specific incident i remember would be when i saw one of the students who appeared to be lost, so i helped him find his friends.
